Statistics

Anxiety

Nearly 20% of Canadian youth aged 12 to 24 reported experiencing a mental health condition, with anxiety disorders being the most common among them.

Depression

About 11% of adolescents have experienced a major depressive episode at some point in their lives, with girls being more than twice as likely as boys to suffer from depression.

ADHD

ADHD affects around 5% of children and youth in Canada, and these individuals often experience significant challenges in academic, social, and personal functioning.

Eating Disorders

Eating disorders have one of the highest mortality rates of any mental illness, with up to 1.5% of young women and 0.3% of young men being affected by conditions such as anorexia nervosa and bulimia nervosa.
